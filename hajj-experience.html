#I. Registration on the Nusuk website

Visit the official Nusuk Hajj portal: https://hajj.nusuk.sa. This is the central platform for managing your Hajj application and approval.

#Registration steps

  • Step 1: Create an account on the Nusuk Hajj portal.
  • Step 2: Upload your required documents (passport and photo).
  • Step 3: Complete the application form.
  • Your application will then be submitted for official verification.

Once submitted, you may need to wait a few days to a few weeks for approval…

Your passport

Get a clear copy of the main biometric page of your passport ready…

  • Accepted file formats: PNG or JPG
  • Maximum dimensions: 800 × 400 pixels
  • Maximum file size: 1 MB

Passport photo

Take a clear photo of yourself using your phone or camera.

  • Format: PNG or JPG
  • Size: 200 × 200 pixels, max 18 KB
  • Background: White
  • Clothing: Modest attire

And if applicable:

  • Your digital copy of your BRP (Biometric Residence Permit)

#Profile verification

Verification typically begins ~6 months before Hajj… Always confirm current dates on official sources.

… سيتم إرسال بريد إلكتروني بعنوان “Audit has been Completed” من العنوان: no_reply@hajj.nusuk.sa

Important notes:

  • If your profile hasn’t been verified yet, don’t worry…
  • If there is an issue, you’ll be asked to correct and resubmit.
  • You can contact support; phone charges apply.

#II. Selecting your Hajj package

Once your profile has been verified, log into Nusuk Hajj and…

#Package types

Price depends on:

  • Service level (Standard / Premium / Luxury)
  • Shifting vs Non-shifting
  • Saudi service provider

  • Flights & transport choices

#Public vs Custom packages

Custom packages

  • Pre-planned itinerary by the provider; travel with the group.

Public packages

  • More flexible; book flights via Mosafer; travel independently to KSA.

#Accommodation type

  • Shifting: hotel near Haram → residence near Mina during Hajj → hotel near Haram after.
  • Non-shifting: same hotel near Haram throughout; harder to access during Mina days.

#Mina camps: Al-Muaisim vs Majar Al-Kabsh

  • Majar Al-Kabsh: ~10-min walk from Jamarat.
  • Al-Muaisim: ~50-min walk from Jamarat.

#Saudi service providers

Al-Muaisim (~50 minutes to Jamarat)

Majar Al-Kabsh (Google Maps shows 24 minutes; usually ~10 if roads are open)

#Cost

Depends on chosen options… 2025 range: ~£5,000–£17,000 pp; premium from ~£6,000 ( 14 days, quadruple room, non-shifting, bus for Makkah to Madina, ... ).

#III. Top up your Nusuk eWallet

  • Activate your eWallet in advance.
  • Check foreign transaction fees.
  • Large transfers: warn your bank; split if needed.
  • Refunds possible (processing fee ~2.6%).
  • Test with a small amount (£50–£100) before topping up fully.

#V. Practical advice

Package sales window — what to expect

  • The most time-sensitive stage is when packages go live on Nusuk.
  • Phased releases (example 2025): custom → public → remaining → re-releases.
  • Windows can be short; places sell fast.

Be ready

  • Get a good internet connection.Sign in early; shortlist packages and prepare your favourite package; ensure your eWallet is ready.
  • UK and Saudi agencies should have outlined the steps and explained each phase. They typically create WhatsApp/Telegram groups you can join for clear guidance. Many YouTube videos also walk through the process in detail ( careful of outdated ones)

Mindset

  • Make du‘ā’; be patient.
  • If successful, perform sujūd to thank Allah. Give a charity (e.g., to the Cambourne Mosque Project :-) ) ...
  • If it doesn’t go through, don’t worry. It may simply not be your turn yet. Stay motivated for later phases or for the coming year, in shā’ Allāh

Communication

  • Typically, your UK agency and/or Saudi agency will create a WhatsApp or Telegram group for travellers on the same package after you get the package. They offer online and in-person training (e.g., in London) on the manāsik (nusuk) of Hajj and the step-by-step process, as well as logistics—accommodation, flights, transport within Saudi Arabia, meals, guides, and other details. Feel free to ask any questions. In our experience, the Saudi agency Rawaf was very responsive in the WhatsApp group and answered queries promptly. Don’t miss their online Q&A sessions—they’re very helpful.

#Children, Elderly & Accessibility — Hajj Guidance

Children (Hajj 2025)

  • Children under 12 are not accepted for Hajj 2025. The organising body (Saudi Ministry of Hajj & Umrah) said this step is “to ensure the safety and well-being of children and to mitigate any potential harm during the Hajj pilgrimage.”
  • Check future seasons for updates, as rules can change year to year.

Travelling with Elderly Parents

  • Be realistic: Hajj with elderly parents is challenging and requires considerable patience and energy. They may depend on you for nearly everything—remember the reward for serving them.
  • Fitness matters: Hajj is physically demanding and should be performed by those fit enough to complete the rites.

Obligation (Fard) & Ability

  • The Qur’an obligates Hajj only on those who are physically and financially capable.
  • If someone has a chronic illness or permanent disability that prevents them from performing Hajj, Hajj is not obligatory for them.
  • If a person is financially able but physically unable, consult a trusted scholar about Hajj al-Badal (proxy Hajj).

Disabled / Differently-Abled Pilgrims

  • Many disabled Muslims can perform Hajj with adequate planning. Discuss your needs with your travel agent before booking.
  • Airports: Jeddah airport provides facilities; ensure your agent notifies airport staff in advance.
  • Masjid al-Haram: Wheelchairs are free; a paid pushing service is available if needed.

Why Hajj Is Hard for Elders (esp. with health issues)

  • Crowds & queues: long toilet lines; incidents can happen when stuck waiting.
  • Mobility & access: Mina → ʿArafah → Muzdalifah → Ḥaram (~48 intense hours) often involves hours of walking. Buses are crowded/slow; wheelchairs aren’t a simple fix.
  • Restricted movement: closures can force long walks to/ from transport.
  • Health stressors: even fit pilgrims often get sick (colds, nosebleeds, exhaustion). Hajj is far tougher than ʿUmrah.

Mina Camp accommodation & facilities

  • Sleeping & luggage: sofa beds in AC halls; space is tight—pack light.

  • Women’s area: separate section; plan meet-ups at the entrance.
  • Meals & refreshments: many UK packages include three meals + snacks.
  • Power & charging: typically one Saudi socket per person.

Travel & logistics

Inter-city: Madinah → Makkah (train vs coach)

  • Coach: ~10 hours (traffic & checks).
  • Train: ~2.5 hours (~£35 pp); punctual—arrive early.
  • Baggage: ~23 kg typical; oversize may go via Saudi Post.

Local transfers

  • Madinah: Uber to station.
  • Makkah: station taxi when Uber wait was long.

Eid day: Jamarat ↔ al-Haram

  • Public shuttle to al-Haram often available; plan return route; train can be faster if buses stall.

Stay & comfort

Muzdalifah (sleeping spot)

  • Avoid sleeping near main doors—constant foot traffic.

Navigation

  • Use Google Maps for navigation during Hajj, especially in Mina.
  • Save key locations (e.g., your Mina camp and tent). Also take a photo of the camp number and your tent number to help you find them later.
  • How to save a place in Google Maps: Tap the spot on the map (your current location or the desired place). On the info card, tap Save. Choose or create a list. Access it later via Saved in the app.
  • Don’t rely on Google Maps 100% in Mina. Road barriers can block the “shortest” route. We were once led to a point separated by a 1.5 m wall between carriageways. People were climbing it; otherwise the detour to the nearest bridge was ~30 minutes. Stay alert and verify routes.
  • Ask for directions and help if needed. Police and volunteers can guide you to bus stations and key sites. Keep asking and cross-checking if answers vary—it helped us find the bus from Jamarat to Masjid al-Haram.
  • Do your homework before you go: collect details on public transport and shuttle services. Having this ready is invaluable, especially in very hot weather.

Toilets & showers

  • Avoid peak times (~30 min before prayers).
  • Mostly squat toilets; seated queues are longer.
  • Bring a waterproof bag to hang clothes; use scent-free soap.

Hydration, heat & sun

  • Hydrate frequently; shower when possible.
  • For walks >20 minutes, carry water and a spray bottle.
  • Use sun cream; sunglasses; UV umbrella.

Medication & medical access

  • Bring enough medication; pharmacies are widespread (possibly not inside Mina camps).
  • Doctors were available in Mina camps for urgent needs.

Medication note: Always read the package leaflet before taking any medication and check for interactions/contraindications. If unsure, consult a pharmacist or doctor.

Ihram & clothing

  • We bought Ihram online; Rawaf Mina also provided one in Madinah.
  • If your Ihram is clean, keep using it; carry a spare just in case.

Footwear, fitness & sleep

  • Break in slippers; walk daily in the 10 days before departure.
  • Earplugs help in Mina if you’re a light sleeper.

Hotels, water & food

  • Makkah (Voco) ~30-minute walk to al-Haram; shuttles stopped as Hajj neared.
  • Water widely available; charities distribute free bottles near al-Haram.

Navigating the Haram (Tawaf by floor)

  • 1st: often best view of the Ka‘bah; 2nd: wheelchair ramp; 2.5: scooters only; 3rd: roof.

Key compliance note

  • During Hajj days, spend more than half the night within Mina’s boundaries.

#Medical advice for pilgrims during Hajj

Personal medical kit

  • Paracetamol, anti-diarrhoeal (e.g., Imodium), plasters & disinfectant (e.g., Betadine), skin-irritation cream (e.g., Bepanthen), sunscreen, lip balm, hand sanitiser, wet wipes, personal medications with prescriptions.

Medication note: Always read the package leaflet before taking any medication. Follow dosing guidance and seek advice if you have other conditions or take regular medicines.

Prevent common illnesses

  • Drink water regularly; sun protection; maintain hygiene.

Foot care

  • Wear open, comfortable, well-broken-in shoes; manage blisters promptly; keep feet dry.

Avoid physical exhaustion

  • Walk at your pace; rest in shade; know heat-stroke signs and tell a guide.

Chronic illnesses

  • Bring enough medication; keep treatment with you; inform a guide.

Diabetic pilgrims using insulin

  • Use insulated pouch for insulin; keep snacks for hypos; monitor sugars; inform a team member.

Medication note: Check the insulin leaflet for storage/temperature limits and dosing instructions. If unsure, ask your clinician.

If you feel unwell

  • Don’t stay alone; notify a guide; go to a medical station promptly.

#Organisational advice for the five days

  • Patience & brotherhood: crowds and heat are part of the test—prioritise respect and support.
  • Follow leaders: stay with your group (Mina → ʿArafah → Muzdalifah).
  • Take turns & stay orderly: queue patiently; carry a small personal kit.
  • Manage effort: steady pace, hydrate, rest.
  • Spiritual focus: turn delays into worship—du‘ā’, dhikr, Qur’an, reflection.
  • Living well in tents: keep noise low, stay tidy, organise belongings, share fairly, accept minor inconveniences with ṣabr.
